the dotcom crash of 2000

 

The dot-com bubble was a historic speculative bubble covering roughly 1997 â€“ 2000 (with a peak in March 2000) during which stock markets in industrialized nations saw their equity value rise rapidly from growth in the Internet sector and related fields.

  • webvan, online grocer that burned through $800 million in funding; bankrupt in 2001
  • boo.com, spent $188m in six months to create an online fashion store; bankrupt in May 2000
  • startups.com, the "ultimate dot-com startup"; out of business in 2002
  • theGlobe.com, a social networking service, largest first day gain of any IPO in history
  • pets.com, pet supplies store famous for puppet mascot and SuperBowl ads; bankrupt in 2000
  • broadcast.com, acquired by Yahoo! for $5.9bn; now defunct
  • GeoCities purchased by Yahoo! for $3.57bn in January 1999; closed ten years later
  • Xcelera.com, a Swedish investor in start-up firms, "Greatest one-year rise stock in history"
  • MicroStrategy, lost more than half its value on a single day following re-stated financials
  • InfoSpace, reached a price $1,305 per share in 2000; crashed down to $22 a share by 2001
  • The Learning Company, bought by Mattel in 1999 for $3.5bn; sold for $27.3 million in 2000
  • Think Tools AG, reached a valuation of CHF 2.5bn in 2000, with no prospects of having a product
