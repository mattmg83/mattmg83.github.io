Economic Darwinism

Gone extinct

 

This section simply lists famous companies or industries that once dominated and have all but gone extinct since, often in dramatic fashion:

  • Print media (Newsweek print edition, News of the World, etc.)
  • Fuller Brushes, the company that started and symbolised door to door sales
  • PanAm and TWA airlines, who fell to plane crashes and the commoditization of air travel
  • Kodak and Polaroid, once icons in photography, who failed in the transition to digital
  • F.W. Woolworth, who fell to the transitiion from Main Street to the shopping mall era
  • Tower Records, who fell from the rise of digital music, both legal and otherwise
  • Circuit City, who fell to online retailer like Amazon.com
  • Blockbuster Video, who fell to online downloads and on-demand videos
